## Product Contract Check

- Pass: the right Intelligence Spine is collapsed by default and stays narrow in first-screen states.
- Pass: the Mission Workspace presents one primary surface at a time: plan, artifact, permission, review, or policy state.
- Pass: the first screen does not read as a dashboard; Mission Rail and Top Command Bar stay quiet and compact.
- Pass: Vela Voice remains available at the bottom of the workspace without covering the mission surface.
- Pass: permission, review, and policy blockers appear in the main workflow, not only inside the Spine.
- Pass: compact layout keeps controls and text legible without obvious overlap.
- Pass: visible shell text is Chinese-first in the reviewed states, while technical identifiers remain where appropriate.

## Observations

- The desktop first screen communicates the active mission, current plan step, next action, input area, Voice layer, and inspection path within the first viewport.
- The artifact workspace is intentionally sparse but still preserves the single-surface rule and gives enough metadata for review.
- Permission and policy states use amber emphasis and direct action buttons, which makes guard decisions hard to miss.
- Review blockers surface both a review action and a completion blocker in the main workflow, matching the autonomy trust rule.
- The compact layout stacks the Voice controls cleanly; the send action remains reachable.

## Remaining Visual Risks

- This sign-off is a manual screenshot review, not a pixel-perfect design baseline.
- The artifact workspace may need richer real artifact previews once live production artifacts replace smoke fixtures.
- A full built-app visual launch still depends on a future platform packaging target.

Vela Phase 5 visual sign-off passes for the current screenshot evidence. No blocking first-screen or hard-rule violations were found.
